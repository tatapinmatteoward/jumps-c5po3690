# Topic 6: The Art of the Pick-and-Roll in Contemporary Basketball

Welcome back to HoopsHaven Central's ongoing series on basketball fundamentals and advanced strategies. In Topic 6, we're zooming in on the pick-and-roll—a timeless play that's been revolutionized in the era of pace-and-space offenses. Whether you're a casual fan or an aspiring coach, understanding this duo's dynamic can elevate your appreciation (or execution) of the game.

## The Basics: What Makes the Pick-and-Roll Tick?

At its core, the pick-and-roll involves a screener (usually a big man) setting a pick on the defender of the ball-handler (often a guard). The ball-handler then uses the screen to drive, pass, or pull up for a shot, while the screener rolls to the basket for a potential dunk or pop-out for a three. It's simple in theory but deadly in practice.

This play has been a staple since the days of John Stockton and Karl Malone, but today's NBA has amplified its effectiveness. With the rise of versatile bigs like Nikola Jokić and versatile guards like Luka Dončić, the pick-and-roll accounts for over 30% of possessions in many teams' playbooks.

## Evolution in the Modern Era

Gone are the days of static screens. Modern iterations incorporate flair like the "Spain" pick-and-roll (with off-ball movement) or the "pistol" action for misdirection. Teams like the Golden State Warriors blend it seamlessly into their motion offense, creating layers of confusion for defenses.

Consider the Phoenix Suns' duo of Chris Paul and Deandre Ayton. Paul's precision passing exploits the roll, forcing defenders into no-win scenarios: switch and get beaten off the dribble, or hedge and leave Ayton open underneath. Stats from the 2022-23 season show pick-and-roll plays boasting an efficiency rating of 1.05 points per possession—higher than the league average.

## Defensive Countermeasures

Defenses aren't sitting idle. The zone pick-and-roll defense, popularized by teams like the Miami Heat, traps the ball-handler and rotates quickly to contest the roll man. Yet, elite offenses adapt by using stagger screens or quick flares to the wing.

For coaches at any level, drilling pick-and-roll defense starts with communication. Teach your bigs to show high on the screen without fully committing, buying time for help rotations.
